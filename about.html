About Us

The goal of the freethinking school's preschool program (1.5 – 6 years) is to unbox a child's talents and creativity. We follow the Montessori method of education that develops the child's five senses through manipulative materials in the classroom, providing for hands-on education. These materials are arranged in a progression of graduating difficulty, allowing the child to progress at his own pace.

Dr. Maria Montessori described ages 3 to 6 as the second phase of the 'sensitive period'. This is a time of enormous developmental leaps, when children are ready to expand their vocabulary, grasp math concepts, and develop habits for working with others.

This program is split into 3 levels. After completing level 3, the children are eligible to enter Class 1.






❝ The education of even a small child, therefore, does not aim at preparing him for school, but for life. ❞

— Dr. Maria Montessori






Our Difference

Where do we learn the lessons that we need for life? Is it in the classroom? Or by observing firsthand through field trips, talks by lifelong experts in a field, a customised curriculum that draws from all fields for a balanced viewpoint, or even themes that link together supposedly random topics to draw out underlying links and connections. "Experience" is the master teacher that spontaneously ignites a love for learning by doing, observing and experimenting. It is the TFS experience that keeps our little freethinkers actively engaged and enthralled in discovery.




Freethinking Curriculum

With attractive Montessori materials, kids are free to explore, question and reason for themselves. We’ve put together a customized curriculum of various teaching methods to give our kids a holistic learning experience.





Extended Day

For children upwards of 3.5 years, we have divided the day into two halves. While the morning half is focused on core subjects, the second half is time for them to explore their creativity.





Expert Community Teachers

We draw on life experts who work alongside classroom teachers to integrate art, music, dance and physical education through the day to further enhance the child’s experience.





Curated Experiences

Field trips to familiar places such as the local grocery stores, parks, libraries, restaurants etc., build on children’s interests especially since it is a natural extension to classroom learning; intended to expand their knowledge to the next level, where it is applied.





Freethinking Class Spaces

The freethinking space is an authentic and charming to cater to the early childhood program. Within the specially prepared indoor and outdoor environments, children freely engage in constructive activities for practical life, sensorial development, language, math, music, dance and movement. It is a preparation of life, which will benefit the child for years to come. Appropriate technological experiences are utilized as a catalyst in the classroom for improved physical, cognitive and social development.





The Matic Unit Based Experiences

Through our inquiry generating themes children explore and understand a vast body of knowledge, making connections to their prior knowledge, gaining perspectives and offering opportunity for creative thought. These themes transcend boundaries of discipline; which link various subject strands that the topic touches upon, thereby broadening and strengthening their long-term understanding of the key idea.

FAQ


At The Freethinking School, we encourage your curiosity to understand our methodology better. Please do feel free to call or write to us about any query you may have regarding your child's formative education.

How old should my child be before I obtain admission in the Freethinking School?
The minimum age for admission is typically 3 years, but it depends on the specific program. Please contact us for more details.
What are the school timings?
The school runs from 8:30 AM to 3:30 PM, Monday to Friday.
Tell me more about the Freethinking curriculum?
Our curriculum is focused on critical thinking, creativity, and experiential learning. We combine traditional education with innovative methods.
What are the extracurricular activities?
We offer a wide range of extracurricular activities including sports, arts, music, drama, and more to ensure holistic development.
Is there a daycare facility after school?
Yes, we provide a daycare facility for students after school hours.
How is TFS different from other schools?
TFS focuses on personalized learning, critical thinking, and experiential education, allowing students to actively engage in their learning.
What are the facilities available at TFS?
TFS provides state-of-the-art classrooms, sports facilities, libraries, art studios, and computer labs to enhance student learning.
What is your student/teacher ratio?
We maintain a low student-to-teacher ratio to ensure personalized attention to each student. The ratio is typically 12:1.
Does the fees include snacks and lunch?
Yes, the fees cover snacks and lunch provided to all students.
Do you have transport?
Yes, we provide a school bus service for students who need transportation.
What are the safety measures implemented at the school?
We implement a range of safety measures including security personnel, CCTV cameras, first-aid facilities, and safety drills.

Free Mentor

Career Preparatory Experiences




The aim of the FreeMentor program is to offer career preparatory experiences to children. Keeping the various and often confusing career options available in today’s world, this program aims to demystify them to children, so they can make informed choices while choosing subjects at the high school level. Children get a macro level understanding of how a particular industry or business works. Part time internships will also be offered during holidays.

  • Open to age group: 12 - 16 years
  • Timing and details: please check calendar
  • A certificate of completion will be given for this program
